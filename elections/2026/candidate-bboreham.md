## What I have done

My first contribution to Prometheus was in 2017 and I am the 4th-biggest contributor
to `prometheus/prometheus` over its lifetime. Many of these have been optimisations
to reduce memory usage or speed up Prometheus.

I have presented CloudNativeCon Prometheus maintainer sessions, spoken at PromCon
four times, mentored LFX and GSoC students and attended nearly every dev summit.
I also attend the Bug Scrub every Tuesday and follow up on a lot of PRs that might
otherwise get lost.

Downstream I work on Grafana Labs' Mimir and Loki projects which re-use Prometheus code.
In the past I have also been a maintainer of CNCF Cortex and CNI projects.

## What I'll do

I believe a great strength of Prometheus has been that it has stuck to its
core job of metrics collection, storage and queries. I will argue to keep this
focus, and not to expand into lots of new areas.

There is a great opportunity for Prometheus to support OpenTelemetry;
I have pushed for the communities to talk more, and I hope we can deliver
the vast capability of Prometheus exporter and client libraries more easily
to OpenTelemetry adopters.

I would also like to see a more organised response to security issues.

## What I have done

I started contributing in 2015.
While initially, this was around finding ALL the bugs and papercuts, I have quickly transitioned into catherding.
I initially suggested replacing PromDash with Grafana as the default dashboard.

Dan Kohn (RIP), the founder of CNCF, has called me the CEO of Prometheus behind my back repeatedly.
My work is largely community, people, and political: Organizing PromCon, making sure we have people speaking at relevant conferences, and unblocking/getting resources where needed. No idea how many I have given myself.
I have chaired the Prometheus dev summit for a few years until we switched to a rotation model.
I have coordinated our compatibility test suite work in the background, and also engaged with a few projects who made too strong a claim about certain technical aspects of what they did.
I founded and led OpenMetrics until 1.0.
I have tried to improve interoperability between OTel and Prometheus since before it was called OpenTelemetry.

I do like to believe that I am well-connected within LF/CNCF and beyond, and thus able to make things work.

## What I'll do

I want to revive Prometheus. Get new contributors in while retaining focus on being The Best(TM) for metrics.

I want to continue the work on making OTel and Prometheus fully compatible.
There's a window of opportunity to potentially have Prometheus metrics & code as part of an OTel Metrics 2.0, which would be one of the best possible outcomes for Prometheus.

I would continue to advocate for Prometheus to get more resources.
Keeping myself honest, I have seen how CNCF Governing Board and CNCF Technical Oversight Committee work from the inside, and honesty compels me to manage my own and everyone else's expectations on how many resources CNCF will actually assign.
